Digital Signature Certificate at a glance

Digital signature is the most advanced and secure type of electronic signature. One can use it to comply with the most demanding legal and regulatory requirements because it provide the highest levels of assurance about each signer's identity and the authenticity of the documents they sign. Digital signatures use a certificate-based digital ID issued by an accredited Certificate Authority (CA) or Trust Service Provider (TSP), so, when you digitally sign a document, your identity is uniquely linked to you, the signature is bound to the document with encryption, and everything can be verified using underlying technology known as Public Key Infrastructure (PKI). Digital signature is stored in USB token which is a password-protected physical device Usage wise Types of Digital Signature: Class III Individual - uses for company registrations, IT Return E-filing, GST Retun E-Filing, PF/ESI, etc. CLass III Organisational- Uses for E-tendering and participating in E-Auctions

Importance of DSC

Company Incorporation The Digital Signature Certificate is mandatory For Directors/Designated Partners if you are incorporating a Public Limited Company, Private Limited Company, Limited Liability Partnership or One Person Company. [ Class II DSC require ]
ROC Compliance All ROC Form to be signed by Directors/Designated Partners with Digital Signature. [ Class II DSC require ]
Income Tax Return In case of Company it is Mandatory to upload ITR by Digital Signature of Authorised Signatory. [ Class II DSC require ]
Various Income Tax Form Certifications Professionals ( Chartered Accountants) mandatory to certify the forms with digital signature to upload online. [ Class II DSC require ]
Professionals Certifications in ROC Forms CA/CMA/CS mandatory to certify with Digital Signature certain forms to be filed with ROC. [ Class II DSC require ]
Goods and Services Tax GST Registration, Filing of Monthly/Quarterly/Yearly return to be certify by attaching DSC of authorised Signatory/Director/Partner/Owner. [ Class II DSC require ]
Import & Export Code It is mandatory to have DSC while applying for an import & export code. [ Class II DSC require ]
Employee Provident Fund / Employee State Insurance EPF & ESI Registration, Return Filing can be done by using Digital Signature of Employer. [ Class II DSC require ]
E-Tendering or E-Auction A Class 3 Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is essential for companies and organizations that take part or intend to take part in E-Tendering or E-Auction processes on various Government sites

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Digital Signature Certificate?+
A Digital Signature Certificate is an electronic document that uses a digital signature to provide proof of the identity of the signer.
How long does it take to obtain a DSC?+
Typically, it takes about 1-2 business days to obtain a DSC once all necessary documents are submitted.
Who can apply for a DSC?+
Any individual, business entity, or organization can apply for a DSC.
Is a DSC legally valid?+
Yes, a DSC is legally recognized under the Information Technology Act, 2000.
What if I lose my DSC USB token?+
If you lose your DSC USB token, you should immediately revoke your certificate through the Certifying Authority. You will need to apply for a new DSC.
Do I need a Digital Signature for e-filing?+
Yes, a Digital Signature is mandatory for e-filing of income tax returns and other government documents.
Can I use the same DSC for multiple purposes?+
Yes, a DSC can be used for multiple purposes such as signing documents, e-filing, and online transactions.
What happens if my DSC expires?+
If your DSC expires, you will need to renew it by submitting the necessary documents to the Certifying Authority.
