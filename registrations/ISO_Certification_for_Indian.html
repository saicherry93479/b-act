What factors should be considered while choosing an ISO Certification Body?+
It is important to choose a recognized and credible certification body as ISO itself does not provide certification. Consider the following:
  • Evaluate several certification bodies.
  • Check if the certification body uses the relevant CASCO standard.
  • Check if it is accredited. Accreditation provides independent confirmation of competence.

What does ISO stand for?+
ISO stands for "International Organization for Standardization." It is an independent, non-governmental international organization headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland, with 161 national standards bodies as members.
What does IAF stand for?+
IAF stands for “International Accreditation Forum,” which continuously monitors and improves international standards. IAF ISO certifications are accepted worldwide.
What is the validity of ISO certification?+
ISO certificates are valid for 3 years, subject to surveillance audits.
Can I check the authenticity of my ISO Certificate?+
Yes, each certificate has a unique certificate number that can be verified on the accreditation body's website.
What is the cost of ISO Certification?+
The cost depends on various factors, including the type of ISO certification, the certifying body, the nature of the business, the size of the organization, and the level of compliance.
Is price important in ISO Certification?+
Price can be a significant factor, but it should not be the only consideration. Choosing a reputable certifying body with a good track record may come at a higher cost but can provide greater value and customer confidence in your organization.

What is ISO/IEC 27000:2018?+
ISO/IEC 27000:2018 provides an overview of information security management systems (ISMS) and includes terms and definitions commonly used in the ISMS family of standards, applicable to all types and sizes of organizations.
What is ISO/IEC 27005:2011?+
ISO/IEC 27005:2011 provides guidelines for information security risk management and supports the implementation of information security based on a risk management approach.
What is ISO/IEC 27002:2013?+
ISO/IEC 27002:2013 offers guidelines for organizational information security standards and practices, including the selection, implementation, and management of controls based on the organization's information security risk environment.
Is an audit compulsory for ISO Certification?+
Yes, audits are compulsory. There are two types: Initial Certification Audit and Surveillance Audits, which ensure ongoing compliance with the chosen standard.
What criteria should I apply when choosing a certifying and accreditation body?+
Consider factors such as reputation, accreditation, specialization, experience, integrated audit options, flexibility, and language proficiency of the auditors.
