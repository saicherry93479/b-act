GST Compliance in India at a Glance

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a very important part of business in India today. As only one tax applies to all sales of Goods and Services in B2B and B2C transactions.

Post-registration of GST, various compliances are required, including:

  • Tax Computations and ITC Adjustment in Books
  • Monthly Tax Deposit
  • Monthly/Quarterly/Yearly Return Filing
  • Matching and Availing Input Tax Credit (ITC) on Return
  • Checking Updated Balance Status on Electronic Ledger
  • Uploading Correct Invoice Details on GST Portal
  • Amendments in GST Registration
  • Ensuring Correctness of GST Rate and HSN Code
  • GST Accounting and Treatment in Profit & Loss Statement and Balance Sheet
  • Stay Updated with Latest Notifications, Circulars, and Rules
  • Delay in Deposit of Tax Attracts Interest @ 18% per annum

Benefits of On-time GST Compliance

Save Penalty & Interest

On-time filing of returns and deposit of tax within the due date saves heavy penalties and interest.

Safe from Unforeseen Notices

Accuracy in return filing and timely compliances keeps you safe from unwanted departmental notices.

Updated Records

Timely compliances ensure your business transactions and tax records are always updated with the department.

Avoid Any Demand

Accuracy in filing and proper compliances help minimize or avoid tax demands on assessments.
