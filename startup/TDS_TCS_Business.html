At a glance

Overview

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS), Tax Collected at Source (TCS) and Business Income Tax Return (B-ITR) are 3 important Prime Direct Tax Compliance which every Firm/LLP/Company doing business in India has to comply and follow the Income Tax Act and Rules.
TDS deduction rate varies expenditure wise as mentioned in Sectins 192 to 196D of the Income Tax Act.
Tax Collection at Source (TCS) - Under section 206C of the Income Tax Act, tax has to be collected at source on sale of Category A, B, C & D goods.
Non Compliance of Tax deduction and/or Deposit shall attract Interest per month and Non Filing of Return attract per day penalty and Prosecution (Imprisonment).

Non Compliance Impact

Failure to deduct TDS or deduct but fail to pay, the entire amount that was deductible is disallowed u/s 40(a)(ia) for the purpose of computing income taxable income from Business.
Income Tax assessing office charge Income Tax with Interest on such expenditure.

Benefits of Compliance

Save Penalty & Interest

Ontime filing of return, Payment of Tax within due date saves heavy penalty and Interest.

Safe from unforeseen Notice

Accuracy in Return filing, ontime compliances helps to keep your records updated with Income Tax department.

Avoid any Demand

Accuracy in Return filing, proper compliances as per rules and legal provisions helps to minimise or avoid any Tax Demand on Assessment.

Loss Carry forward

Current year's business loss as per Income Tax Act can be carried forward and setoff future income only if ITR file within the due date.

Penalties for Non-Compliance

Non Filing of TDS/TCS Return - Penalty Rs 200 per day per return
Non Deposit or Short Deposit of TDS/TCS - Interest @ 1.5% Per Month
Non Deduction or Short Deduction of TDS/TCS - Interest @ 1% Per Month
Imprisonment - If assessee fails to pay the deducted TDS to the government
Penalty Under Section 271H - Rs 10,000 to Rs 1,00,000
