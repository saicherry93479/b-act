Features of a Public Limited Company (PLC)

Can raise large capital by issuing shares, listed on Stock Exchanges in India.
Minimum 3 Directors and 7 Members/Shareholders required.
Incorporation in 2 days | Includes Share Certificate, MOA/AOA, DSC, DIN, PAN & TAN, etc.
Minimum Paid-up Capital of Rs. 5,00,000/-
A Public Limited Company must include the word 'Limited' after its name.
Issue Prospectus for the public to raise funds.
Public Limited Company can raise funds from the public by issuing shares subject to compliance with the provisions of the Act.
No limitation for the number of Members/shareholders.
A Public Limited Company has all the advantages of a Private Limited Company and can have any number of members, ease in transfer of shareholding, and more transparency.

Pre-requisite for Company Incorporation

Company name must be unique and should not match any existing companies or trademarks.
Proposed Directors must have Active DIN.
Digital Signature for Directors/Shareholders.
Decide Paid-up Share Capital and share % between Shareholders.
Decide Company Registration State and Office address.

Post-Incorporation Compliances

File Commencement of Business (COB) e-form INC 20A within 180 days of incorporation.
Appointment of a Statutory Auditor for auditing books of accounts and issue of audited Annual Financial Statement.
Filing of ROC Annual Forms and Director e-KYC.
If listed, comply with SEBI, Stock Exchange and ROC provisions.

Why you may need Public Limited Company?

Raising capital is much easier for a public limited company through public subscription as well as stock market (if listed)
As the number of shareholders is quite large, the risk borne by each shareholder is considerably low.
Directors and Shareholders have no personal liabilities. Limited Liability protects the personal wealth of the shareholders and directors therefore owners will never lose more that they have invested.
A public company can get its shares listed in a stock exchange therefore; people will be able to easily and quickly recognize the brand or name of the company. The more brand recognition a company has, the more business it will have.
Management of the company is not confined to few persons. Shareholders have powers to elect and remove directors and they can exercise control over management in general meetings of the company.

DIN is a unique identification number issued to a director of a company, required for all directors in India to register with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA).

DSC is an electronic equivalent of a handwritten signature, used for online filing and authentication of company documents during the registration process.
