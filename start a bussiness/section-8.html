Frequently asked questions

What is a Non-Profit company?

A Non-Profit Company to promote art, science, charity, religion, or commerce for social development, social welfare, and charity can be registered under Section 8 of the Indian Companies Act. But remember, the profits, if any, cannot be divided among its members/directors.

What are the documents required for Registration of Non-Profit Company?

For Section 8 company registration, the following documents are essential: The name of the Company for approval, Address proof of the office (electricity or water bill or house tax receipt), Identity proof of all the Directors (Driving License, Passport, Voter ID, Aadhaar card), Memorandum of Association, and Articles of Association.

What type of company is a Non-Profit Company?

A Section 8 Company is a company registered under the Companies Act, primarily formed to promote art, science, charity, religion, commerce, social welfare, and other charitable causes, with no intention of earning profits for its members.

What does it mean by Non-Profit?

A non-profit refers to an organization or company that operates without the goal of distributing profits among its members. Instead, any surplus income is reinvested to further the company's charitable or social goals.

What are the documents required for Registration of Non-Profit Companies?

For Section 8 company registration, the essential documents are: The name of the Company for approval, Address proof (electricity/water bill or house tax receipt), Identity proof of all Directors (Driving License, Passport, Voter ID, Aadhaar card), Memorandum of Association, and Articles of Association.

What is the time taken to register a Non-Profit Company?

The registration of a Non-Profit Company typically takes around 7-10 business days, depending on the completion of the required documentation and approval processes.
