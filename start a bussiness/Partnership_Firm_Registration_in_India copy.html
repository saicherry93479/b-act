Frequently Asked Questions

What is Partnership?+
A partnership is a business structure where two or more individuals agree to share profits and losses of a business carried on by all or any of them acting for all. It's governed by the Indian Partnership Act, 1932.
What are the documents required for Partnership Registration?+
The essential documents include PAN Card of all partners, passport-size photographs, Aadhar Card, bank statements or utility bills as address proof, business address proof, and a properly executed Partnership Deed.
Who can apply for Partnership Registration?+
Any two or more individuals who are of legal age (18 years or above), mentally competent, and not disqualified by law can form a partnership. Non-Indian citizens can also be partners, subject to applicable regulations.
Is Partnership Registration Compulsory?+
While registration of a partnership firm is not mandatory under the Partnership Act, it is highly recommended. Registration provides legal recognition and various benefits including the ability to file legal suits against third parties and other partners.
What is Partnership Deed?+
A Partnership Deed is a legal document that outlines the rights, duties, and obligations of all partners. It includes details about profit sharing, capital contribution, management responsibilities, and other terms and conditions governing the partnership.
When can Partnership be terminated?+
A partnership can be terminated in several situations: by mutual agreement of all partners, on completion of the venture, on death or insolvency of a partner, by giving notice in case of partnership at will, or through a court order in case of misconduct or breach of agreement.
What are the benefits of getting Partnership Registered?+
Registration offers several benefits including: legal recognition of the firm, right to file legal suits against third parties, protection of property rights, easier access to bank loans and credit, ability to claim set-off in proceedings, and enhanced credibility with stakeholders.
