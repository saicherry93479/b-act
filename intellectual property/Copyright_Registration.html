Copyright at a Glance

Copyright, a form of intellectual property law, protects original works of authorship including literary, dramatic, musical, and artistic works, such as poetry, novels, photographs, paintings, speeches, movies, songs, source code of a software program, the binary executable file, websites, mobile apps, and architecture.

Its registration with the IPR Authority essentially ensures that any creative work cannot be copied without the permission of the author or creator. This allows the author to charge others for copying, modifying, or building upon their work.

By registering your work, you protect your rights and allow for potential earnings by licensing it to others.

Know the Meaning of Different Status of Copyright Application

Work Awaited: Work yet to be received.
Waiting: Application in mandatory waiting period of one month.
Documents not received: Documents not received after making payment.
Abandoned: Documents not received after filing.
Scrutiny: Application is under process.
Registered: ROC is generated and will be sent to you by registered post.

What is the scope of protection in the Copyright Act, 1957?+
The Copyright Act, 1957 protects original literary, dramatic, musical, and artistic works from unauthorized uses.
Can I protect my ideas, procedures, methods, or mathematical concepts under the Copyright Act, 1957?+
No, there is no copyright protection for ideas, procedures, methods of operation, or mathematical concepts as such.
Is it necessary to register a work to claim copyright?+
No, copyright comes into existence as soon as a work is created. However, registration serves as prima facie evidence in a court of law regarding ownership disputes.
Where can I file an application for registration of copyright for a work?+
The Copyright Office is located at Plot no. 32, Boudhik Sampada Bhawan, Sector 14, Dwarka, New Delhi- 110075. Applications are accepted by post and online through the E-filing facility.
Are unpublished works registered?+
Yes, both published and unpublished works can be registered. Unpublished works require a copy of the manuscript to be submitted.
Can computer software or programs be registered?+
Yes, computer software or programs can be registered as 'literary works' under the Copyright Act, 1957.
How can I get copyright registration for my website?+
A website may consist of various copyrightable components. Separate applications must be submitted for each component work/content appearing on a website.
